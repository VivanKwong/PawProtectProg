Animal Habitat Statistics

Approximately 3 species per hour are being pushed to extinction (30,000 a year).

The main threat for 85% of these species is habitat loss.

Each year, hunters in the US shoot 4,000 tons of lead into the environment, resulting in the lead poisoning deaths of an estimated 20 million animals.

80% of the decline in global biological diversity is caused by habitat destruction, which is happening at a rate of 240 acres per hour (over 2.1 million a year).

100 million animals die each year as a result of water pollution, and 1,000 species of marine animals are affected by ocean contamination..
